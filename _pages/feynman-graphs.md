In the course of my work I have to evaluate various Feynman integrals analytically. 
I use the framework of variation of mixed Hodge structures and the Feynman integrals arise as relative periods. They show interesting connections with mirror symmetry, number theory and the theory of D-modules, which can be used for concrete (analytical or numerical) evaluations of the Feynman integrals appearing in particle physics or gravity.

## Methods

The methods I use are based on algebraic geometry seeing the Feynman integrals as relative periods of mixed Hodge structures

### Motivic Period integrals 

+ [The elliptic dilogarithm for the sunset graph](https://arxiv.org/abs/1309.5865) published [J.Number Theor. 148 (2015) 328](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0022314X14003278)
+ [A Feynman integral via higher normal functions](https://arxiv.org/abs/1406.2664) published in  [Compos.Math. 151 (2015) 2329](https://www.cambridge.org/core/journals/compositio-mathematica/article/abs/feynman-integral-via-higher-normal-functions/227E87159E99550F35E226A127DED5F4)
+ [Local mirror symmetry and the sunset Feynman integral](https://arxiv.org/abs/1601.08181) published in [Adv.Theor.Math.Phys. 21 (2017) 1373](https://link.intlpress.com/JDetail/1805560840512282625)

### Hodge structure
+ [Motivic geometry of two-loop Feynman integrals](https://arxiv.org/abs/2302.14840) published in [Quart.J.Math.Oxford Ser. 75 (2024) 901](https://academic.oup.com/qjmath/article-abstract/75/3/901/7690665?redirectedFrom=fulltext)

### Picard-Fuchs equations
+ [Algorithms for minimal Picard-Fuchs operators of Feynman integrals](https://arxiv.org/abs/2209.10962) published in [Lett.Math.Phys. 113 (2023) 37](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s11005-023-01661-3)
